About

A community library in Harare, Zimbabwe

Background

Paivapo Institution was founded by artist Felix Shumba. A community library is not a luxury — it is a necessity. In Zimbabwe, access to books on art, ecology, and critical thought remains uneven. We exist to address that.

The name Paivapo means presence and place. It is a declaration: here, where we are. We are rooted in Harare, but our collection is open to all. We collect, preserve, and share books that are rigorous, considered, and purposeful.

Our founding collection of approximately 100 titles spans three areas: art, ecology, and literature. These represent how we understand the world — through making, through living systems, and through language.

Our London office handles book donations and international logistics. The result is straightforward: a place where a student, an artist, a farmer, or a stranger can walk in, sit down, and read.
