The story of Paivapo Institution

Our Story

Paivapo Institution was founded by artist Felix Shumba with a singular conviction: that a community library is not a luxury but a necessity. In a landscape where access to books — especially on art, ecology, and critical thought — remains uneven, Paivapo exists to close that gap.

The name Paivapo speaks to presence and place. It is a declaration: here, where we are. The institution is rooted in Harare, Zimbabwe, but its vision extends beyond geography. We collect, preserve, and share books that matter — works that challenge, illuminate, and inspire.

Our founding collection of approximately 100 titles spans three disciplines: art, ecology, and literature. These are not arbitrary categories — they represent the interconnected ways humans understand the world: through making, through living systems, and through story.

With support from our London office, which handles book donations and international logistics, Paivapo is building something enduring. A place where a student, an artist, a farmer, or a curious stranger can walk in, sit down, and read.

Paivapo Institution will be represented at the Venice Biennale 2026 through the work of artist Felix Shumba. This participation reflects the institution's commitment to bringing Zimbabwean cultural production into global conversation.
